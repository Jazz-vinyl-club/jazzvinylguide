# Maiden Voyage on Vinyl: The Definitive Pressing Guide

## Summary

*Maiden Voyage* was recorded on **March 17, 1965**, at Van Gelder Studio in Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey. Personnel: Herbie Hancock (piano), Freddie Hubbard (trumpet), George Coleman (tenor saxophone), Ron Carter (bass), Tony Williams (drums). Produced by Alfred Lion. Released April 1965 on Blue Note; inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame in 1999. The album — all Hancock originals — is built around a single sustained nautical metaphor. Its opening title track became a jazz standard; "Dolphin Dance" is among the most studied piano compositions in jazz pedagogy. *Maiden Voyage* is Hancock's finest achievement as a Blue Note leader and one of the most recorded albums in the entire Blue Note catalogue for its compositional depth.

The original Blue Note New York USA pressings — mono and stereo — are the collector benchmark. The stereo is genuinely excellent here: Tony Williams's drumming spreads naturally across the soundfield and Hubbard's trumpet placement is ideal. Music Matters produced a definitive 33rpm AAA version; the Blue Note Classic Vinyl is the accessible entry point. LondonJazzCollector notes the Classic Vinyl series as "lifeless" compared to originals, which is a useful reminder that mono fold-downs are not native mono — both the mono and stereo originals derive from native tapes.

**Best early pressing for audiophiles:** [Original Blue Note BST 84195 stereo, New York USA label](https://www.discogs.com/release/1907831-Herbie-Hancock-Maiden-Voyage) – deep groove (Side A), Van Gelder stamp, Plastylite ear.

**Best modern pressing for audiophiles:** [Music Matters 33rpm AAA stereo 180g](https://www.discogs.com/release/4354025-Herbie-Hancock-Maiden-Voyage) – Kevin Gray, QRP, gatefold tip-on.

**Best-value modern:** [Blue Note Classic Vinyl BST 84195 180g (2021)](https://www.discogs.com/release/18566068-Herbie-Hancock-Maiden-Voyage) – Kevin Gray, Optimal, 180g, ~$25.


## Recording history and tape provenance

Van Gelder at Englewood Cliffs in 1965 was operating at the peak of his Blue Note period. The March 1965 date followed Hancock's *Empyrean Isles* (1964) and represented a further shift toward a more open, harmonically spacious style. Alfred Lion gave Hancock considerable latitude; all five tracks are Hancock originals, and the session has the feel of a composer presenting a unified work rather than a collection of set pieces.

**Mono vs stereo:** Unlike some earlier Blue Note titles where the stereo was a rechanneled fold-down, Van Gelder by 1965 was running parallel mono and stereo recording chains. Both are native to their format. The stereo is often preferred for *Maiden Voyage* specifically because Williams's drums open up across the right channel in a way that enhances the rhythm section's contribution. The mono has more concentrated midrange weight. LJC's preference for the original over modern reissues is a reasonable position for the collector; the modern Kevin Gray cuts are excellent for most listeners.

**Tape provenance:** The original Alfred Lion–era tapes are in the Blue Note/Universal archive. Both Music Matters and the Classic Vinyl series sourced from the original analogue master tapes.

## Original US Blue Note pressings

**Catalogue: BLP 4195** (mono) / **BST 84195** (stereo)

### First pressing – New York USA label

The New York USA label pressings (1965–67) are the original and correct target:

- **"Blue Note Records Inc. • New York USA"** on labels
- **Van Gelder stamp** in the deadwax
- **Plastylite ear** in the deadwax
- Deep groove on Side A label (typical of mid-1965 Blue Note pressings)
- Matrix: **BNLP-4195-A VAN GELDER P** / **BNLP-4195-B VAN GELDER P** (mono)
- Stereo matrix: **BNST-84195-A VAN GELDER P** / **BNST-84195-B VAN GELDER P**

**NM originals: $200–$500. VG+: $100–$300.**

### Liberty-era pressings (1966–1971)

Labels read "A Division of Liberty Records Inc." Van Gelder stamps remain on many. Affordable alternative. **$40–$100 VG+.**

## The reissue hierarchy

### Music Matters 33rpm AAA stereo 180g – the pre-Classic Vinyl benchmark

**Cat. MM33-84195** | Format: 180g LP, 33⅓, stereo, gatefold tip-on jacket. **Mastered by Kevin Gray at Cohearent Audio, AAA, from the original stereo master tape. Pressed at QRP.** Music Matters' *Maiden Voyage* is consistently praised and now out of print. Secondary market prices have risen. **$150–$250 secondary.**

### Blue Note Classic Vinyl 180g (2021) – the recommended modern option

**Cat. BST 84195** (or ST-84195) | Format: 180g LP, 33⅓, stereo. **Mastered by Kevin Gray at Cohearent Audio. Pressed at Optimal in Germany.** The accessible modern option. LJC describes the reissue series as "lifeless" in comparison to originals, which holds for listeners on revealing systems — but for the majority of setups, the Classic Vinyl is an excellent pressing. **$20–$35 new.**

---

## Pressings to avoid

**Liberty/UA pressings without Van Gelder stamp:** Some later pressings were cut from dubs rather than original metalwork. Inspect deadwax before purchasing any non-original copy.

**Generic 1970s–80s reissues:** Not recommended when the Classic Vinyl is available at a comparable or lower price.

## Buyer's guide by budget

**Under $35:** The [**Blue Note Classic Vinyl BST 84195**](https://www.discogs.com/release/18566068) — Kevin Gray mastering, Optimal pressing. The recommended starting point.

**$150–$250:** The [**Music Matters 33rpm AAA**](https://www.discogs.com/release/4354025) if found at a reasonable secondary price — worth pursuing for its native stereo AAA mastering.

**$200–$500:** An original [**New York USA BST 84195 stereo**](https://www.discogs.com/master/32314-Herbie-Hancock-Maiden-Voyage) in honest VG+ or better, with Van Gelder stamp and Plastylite ear confirmed.
