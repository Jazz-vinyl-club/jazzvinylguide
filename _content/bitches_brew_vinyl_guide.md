# Bitches Brew on Vinyl: The Definitive Pressing Guide

## Summary

*Bitches Brew* was recorded over three consecutive days – **August 19–21, 1969** – at Columbia's Studio B in New York City. Davis assembled the musicians on very short notice with minimal written material: Joe Zawinul, Wayne Shorter, and Bennie Maupin (woodwinds); Chick Corea, Larry Young, and Joe Zawinul (electric pianos); John McLaughlin (guitar); Dave Holland and Harvey Brooks (basses); Jack DeJohnette, Lenny White, and Don Alias (drums/percussion); Airto Moreira and Juma Santos (further percussion). Davis directed from within the ensemble. What producer Teo Macero then did in the editing room – using tape loops, splices, echo, and the custom "Teo-I" echo unit – assembled those raw performances into a composed work. Neither Bennie Maupin nor Joe Zawinul recognized the finished album when they first heard it.

The tape history has direct consequences for vinyl: by the 1990s, Macero found oxidation and missing sections when pulling tapes for the Complete Sessions box, and some reconstruction was required. This deterioration, combined with the DSD-to-lathe chain used by Mobile Fidelity (disclosed since 2022), and the question of whether MoFi used the original 1970 mix or the 1998 box-set remix, make tape provenance more contested here than on most Columbia jazz titles. The original 1970 two-eye pressings – particularly with low matrix numbers – remain the best case for hearing the unmediated original mix from period vinyl.

**Best early pressing for audiophiles:** [Original US two-eye 360 Sound, low matrix -1J on all four sides](https://www.discogs.com/release/37006-Miles-Davis-Bitches-Brew) – red 360 Sound labels, GP 26, Terre Haute or Pitman plant.

**Best modern pressing for audiophiles:** [Mobile Fidelity UltraDisc One-Step 55th Anniversary 2LP (2025)](https://www.discogs.com/master/8260-Miles-Davis-Bitches-Brew) – Krieg Wunderlich, DSD-to-lathe, Fidelity Record Pressing; 5,000 numbered copies.

**Best-value modern:** [Mobile Fidelity MFSL 2-439 180g 2LP (2014/2023)](https://www.discogs.com/release/5804415-Miles-Davis-Bitches-Brew) – same Wunderlich mastering; widely available at $50–$80.


## Recording history and tape provenance

Davis gave the musicians "musical sketches" – a chord, a mode, a mood, a tempo – and directed from within the ensemble, snapping his fingers for tempo cues or pointing at soloists. The recording was entirely improvised in the moment; Macero then spent months editing the tapes. "Pharaoh's Dance" contains 19 edits; the title track 15. Macero used tape loops (short sections repeated to create new themes), reverb chambers, and the "Teo-I" – a tape-echo device built for him by Columbia technicians. "Miles Runs the Voodoo Down" is the only track presented without any post-session editing. The studio work was as much composition as engineering.

**The 1998 remix question:** The 1998 Complete Bitches Brew Sessions box set includes a remixed version of the album, not simply a remaster. When preparing it, Macero found that the master tapes had physically deteriorated – "actual sections of tape were missing because of oxidation" – and some reconstruction was necessary. Whether MoFi's 2014 and 2025 pressings use the original 1970 two-track mix or the 1998 reconstruction is not definitively stated by MoFi. They state mastering from "the original master tapes" via a 1/4" / 15 IPS analog master to DSD 64 chain. Buyers who specifically want the original 1970 mix as Davis and Macero first issued it should prioritise the original pressing or early reprints.

**The DSD disclosure:** Mobile Fidelity's mastering chain for this title is: analog master tape → DSD 64 digital capture → analog console → lathe. This was confirmed in 2022 as part of the broader MoFi disclosure. The DSD step is defended by MoFi as a transfer tool that allows them to work with deteriorating tapes without repeated playback; critics argue it introduces a sonic ceiling.

## Original US Columbia pressings (1970)

Released **March 30, 1970.** Catalogue: **Columbia GP 26** (double LP gatefold).

- Format: double LP, gatefold with inner gatefold panel
- Cover art: Mati Klarwein painting (also used on Santana's *Abraxas*) on front; gatefold interior has further Klarwein artwork
- Full title on front cover: "Directions In Music By Miles Davis / Miles Davis Bitches Brew"

### The 360 Sound two-eye label

The earliest pressings use the **red "360 Sound Stereo" two-eye label** – named for the two CBS "eye" logos flanking "360 Sound." Columbia introduced the red/orange ring label later the same year, so true two-eye copies represent an early production window.

**Key identification:**
- Two CBS eye logos either side of "360 Sound STEREO"
- **GP 26** on labels and cover (later pressings may show PG 26 on cover while retaining GP 26 on labels, or vice versa)
- Interior gatefold: "Manufactured by Columbia Records/CBS, Inc./51 W. 52 Street, New York, N.Y."

**Pressing plant identification:**

Columbia's three US plants were all used for this title. Plant identification comes from stamped letters in the deadwax:
- **"P"** stamp = **Pitman, New Jersey**
- **"T"** stamp = **Terre Haute, Indiana**  
- **"S"** (backwards) = **Santa Maria, California**

The letter suffix in the matrix (e.g., -1A, -1B, -1C) does not reliably indicate pressing plant; this is a commonly repeated misconception. According to a Columbia cutting engineer quoted on AnalogPlanet, the alpha suffix indicated lacquer sequence for distribution to multiple plants simultaneously, not generation order: "An initial lacquer order was 6 sets plus a ref for QC... 1A&B went to Pitman (N.J.), 1C&D to Terre Haute, (IN), 1E&F went to Santa Maria (CA)." The plant stamp is the reliable identifier.

**Matrix guide:** The collector consensus for earliest and best-sounding pressings is **-1J on all four sides** (XSM 152412 through 152415). A Popsike seller with deep knowledge of this title states: "If you really, truly want that revolutionary, amazingly expansive, wide-open feel and sharp soundstage separation... you want nothing later than '-1J' ending all 4 matrix numbers." The -1J matrices were cut early and circulate on both Pitman and Terre Haute pressings.

**Sanctuary songwriter credit error:** The earliest two-eye pressings mistakenly credit both Wayne Shorter and Miles Davis as songwriters for "Sanctuary." The corrected version (Shorter only) appears on later two-eye copies. The error pressing is the earliest state.

**Mixed-label curiosity:** At least one documented copy exists where Record 1 has the black-and-white two-eye labels and Record 2 has the later red/orange labels. This transition-period hybrid is the result of simultaneous pressing runs.

**Market values:** NM two-eye with low matrices: **$150–$400**. VG++: **$60–$120**. Gatefold covers prone to seam splits and corner wear; inspect carefully.

### GP 26 vs. PG 26

The original catalogue number is **GP 26** ("Gatefold Package"). Columbia later changed the prefix to **PG 26**. Copies with PG 26 on the cover are reprints, though some transitional copies have PG 26 on the cover and GP 26 on the labels.

### Red/orange "Columbia" ring label (1970–75)

The red/orange ring label was introduced almost simultaneously with *Bitches Brew*'s release. Early red/orange pressings from 1970–72 with low matrix suffixes can sound very close to two-eye originals. One collector who owns both a two-eye and a later orange/red repress: "I can't really tell them apart." These trade for **$25–$60** and represent strong value.

## The reissue hierarchy

### Mobile Fidelity MFSL 2-439 180g 2LP (2014/2023) – standard modern reference

**Cat. MFSL 2-439** | Format: 2×180g, 33⅓, gatefold, numbered. **Mastered by Krieg Wunderlich, GAIN 2 Ultra Analog System, Sebastopol, CA. Source: 1/4" / 15 IPS analog master → DSD 64 → analog console → lathe. Pressed at RTI (2014 run); Fidelity Record Pressing (2023 run).** Original run: 10,000 numbered copies; subsequently repressed.

Matrix confirms "KW@MoFi." The DSD intermediate step is now disclosed. The results are widely praised despite the debate: "Really good pressing. For the money, I think it's one of the best pressings of this album." One Discogs owner who also has a 1981 Japanese reissue notes the Japanese sounds "superior." The Skeptical Audiophile/Better Records' hot stamper community finds vintage originals preferable. This is the accessible modern default. **$50–$80 on the secondary market.**

### Mobile Fidelity UltraDisc One-Step 55th Anniversary 2LP (2025) – current summit

**Cat. MFSL UD1S-109** | Format: 2×180g, 33⅓, box set, **5,000 numbered copies. Same DSD64 mastering chain by Krieg Wunderlich. Pressed at Fidelity Record Pressing on 180g black vinyl** – MoFi auditioned multiple vinyl formulations and chose 180g black over MoFi SuperVinyl for this title specifically.

The One-Step process bypasses the standard three-step plating (father → mother → stamper) by cutting stampers directly from lacquers, giving a lower noise floor and finer groove resolution. MoFi explains the black vinyl choice: "After closely auditioning Bitches Brew on several different vinyl profiles... the music on this 1970 album translates with superior definition, clarity, presence, dynamics, and balance on this format." **~$125–$150 new where available.**

### Japanese CBS/Sony pressings

Japanese CBS/Sony pressings of this title from the early 1980s receive consistent praise for pressing quality. One Discogs owner: "Listening to this one now immediately after hearing the superior 1981 Japan reissue." The Tracking Angle's Miles Davis collecting guide singles out the Sony Shizuoka pressing plant as "the absolute best in the world... even better than RTI or QRP." CBS/Sony Japan cut their masters locally and pressed at Shizuoka, resulting in dead-quiet vinyl.

Key identifiable Japanese pressings include:
- **20AP 1425/26** (early 1980s): Standard CBS/Sony Japan, orange/red labels, double LP, with obi
- Earlier single-LP CBS/Sony Japan issues with the original artwork

These trade for **$50–$120 with obi strip** and are consistently rated superior to the MoFi by several experienced collectors.

### Columbia Legacy editions

Columbia/Legacy has issued several anniversary editions digitally sourced. The 40th Anniversary (2010) and Legacy studio-series reissues are acceptable entry points at $25–$35. The Tracking Angle: a 2016 Legacy pressing "wasn't as far behind" the MoFi as expected, with "clearer mids, less distortion in the highs and texture in the bass" as the MoFi's advantage.

## Buyer's guide by budget

**Under $40:** A clean early **red/orange Columbia ring label (1970–72)** with low matrix numbers – analogue, period vinyl, close to the two-eye in sound. The most practical original-era option.

**$50–$80:** The **MoFi MFSL 2-439** – the modern reference, widely available, broadly praised.

**$80–$150:** A clean **two-eye 360 Sound original** with low matrices and the Sanctuary error. The analog purist's choice.

**$50–$120:** A **Japanese CBS/Sony 1980s pressing with obi** – often rated above the MoFi by direct comparison; Shizuoka pressing quality is exceptional.

**$125–$150:** The **MoFi UltraDisc One-Step 55th Anniversary** (if available) – lowest noise floor, best pressing quality in the MoFi lineage.
