# Speak No Evil on Vinyl: The Definitive Pressing Guide

## Summary

*Speak No Evil* was recorded on **Christmas Eve, December 24, 1964**, at Van Gelder Studio in Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey. Personnel: Wayne Shorter (tenor saxophone), Freddie Hubbard (trumpet), Herbie Hancock (piano), Ron Carter (bass), Elvin Jones (drums). Produced by Alfred Lion; released April 1966 on Blue Note. The album is among the most perfectly realised hard bop statements on record — every soloist at or near their peak, Van Gelder capturing it in the warm, deep-focus mono and stereo that defines the 1964–65 Blue Note period. The cover photograph features Shorter's then-wife Teruko (Irene) Nakagami.

Speak No Evil is one of the Blue Note titles where both mono and stereo are genuinely excellent. The mono presents the ensemble as a unified mass with uncanny intimacy; the stereo opens the soundstage and separates Elvin Jones's polyrhythmic drumming with exceptional clarity. Original New York USA pressings — with the Plastylite ear and Van Gelder stamp — are the collector standard, but Music Matters and the Blue Note Classic Vinyl reissue both offer close approximations for the listening collector.

**Best early pressing for audiophiles:** [Original Blue Note BST 84194 stereo, New York USA label](https://www.discogs.com/release/4088476-Wayne-Shorter-Speak-No-Evil) – deep groove, Van Gelder stamp, Plastylite ear.

**Best modern pressing for audiophiles:** [Music Matters 33rpm AAA mono (2015)](https://www.discogs.com/release/7248785-Wayne-Shorter-Speak-No-Evil) – Kevin Gray, 180g, QRP.

**Best-value modern:** [Blue Note Classic Vinyl BST 84194 (2020/2021)](https://www.discogs.com/release/16941774-Wayne-Shorter-Speak-No-Evil) – Kevin Gray, Optimal, 180g; minor QC variation, check reviews.


## Recording history and tape provenance

Van Gelder had been in the Englewood Cliffs facility since 1959, and by 1964 the studio was producing the most sophisticated recordings of his career. The Christmas Eve session caught Shorter at the exact moment he was fully coming into his own compositional voice — all five tracks are Shorter originals, and the album stands as his definitive Blue Note statement. Alfred Lion's production approach — allowing time for rehearsal, capturing takes that had the energy of live performance — is fully evident.

**Mono vs stereo:** Unlike many Blue Note titles from 1959–1963 where the stereo was a secondary fold-down from the mono tape, Van Gelder was by 1964 running parallel mono and stereo recording chains. Both versions derive from their own native tape. The stereo presentation is wide and natural, with Elvin Jones's drums in full detail across the right channel and the horn soloists front-centre. The mono has a concentrated, pressurised quality that many collectors prefer for the rhythm section. Neither is categorically superior; the debate is genuine.

**Tape provenance:** The original Alfred Lion–era tapes are in the Blue Note/Universal archive and have been the source for all credentialled reissues including Music Matters and the Blue Note Classic Vinyl series. Music Matters worked directly from the original mono and stereo master tapes.

## Original US Blue Note pressings

**Catalogue: BLP 4194** (mono) / **BST 84194** (stereo)

### First stereo pressing – New York USA label

The most sought-after first pressings carry the **"Blue Note Records Inc. • New York USA"** label (the address format used 1964–67 before the Liberty acquisition):

- Orange/blue or black/white label depending on pressing run
- **Plastylite ear** (the "P" etching) in the deadwax
- **VAN GELDER** stamped in the deadwax
- Deep groove labels
- Matrix: **BNST-84194-A VAN GELDER P** / **BNST-84194-B VAN GELDER P**
- Laminated gatefold sleeve

These are the sonic benchmark. **NM stereo originals: $200–$500. VG+: $100–$250.**

### First mono pressing – BLP 4194

The mono is slightly less sought-after than the stereo on the secondary market but equally important sonically. Same New York USA label period, same Van Gelder/Plastylite deadwax authentication. **NM: $200–$450. VG+: $100–$200.**

### Liberty and United Artists era (1966–1971)

After Liberty Records acquired Blue Note in 1966, pressings show "A Division of Liberty Records Inc." on the label. Van Gelder stamps remain on many of these, and the sonic quality is considered only marginally inferior to the New York USA originals. Much more affordable. **$30–$80 VG+.**

## The reissue hierarchy

### Music Matters 33rpm 180g mono (2015) – the audiophile benchmark

**Cat. MM33-84194** | Format: 180g LP, 33⅓, gatefold tip-on jacket. **Mastered by Kevin Gray at Cohearent Audio, AAA, from the original mono master tape. Pressed at QRP.** The Music Matters Speak No Evil is one of the highlights of the label's Blue Note series — the mono tape is in superb condition and Gray's cut emphasises the weight of the ensemble. Widely regarded as the finest available version of the mono presentation.

**$150–$250 secondary; occasionally in print.**

### Blue Note Classic Vinyl 180g (2020/2021) – the accessible modern option

**Cat. BST 84194** | Format: 180g LP, 33⅓. **Mastered by Kevin Gray at Cohearent Audio, pressed at Optimal in Germany.** The Blue Note Classic Vinyl series produced a number of excellent pressings; the Speak No Evil is among the better ones. Some copies have reported QC issues (slight pressing marks, minor surface noise variation) — inspect before buying if possible. At under $30 new, it is the recommended entry point. **$20–$35 new.**

### Blue Note 85th Anniversary blue vinyl (2024)

**Cat. 5859221** | Limited to 3,000 copies. 180g blue vinyl reissue; coloured vinyl pressing with same Kevin Gray cut as the Classic Vinyl series. A collectible curiosity. **$40–$60.**

## Pressings to avoid

**Liberty-era stereo without Van Gelder stamp:** Some 1960s Liberty/United Artists pressings were cut from dubs rather than the original master metal — check the deadwax for the VAN GELDER stamp. Pressings without it are sonic downgrades.

**Generic European reissues (Stateside, various):** UK and European pressings of this title were issued on various sub-labels without access to original US metalwork. Sound varies considerably and most are not recommended over the Classic Vinyl at its current price.

## Buyer's guide by budget

**Under $35:** The [**Blue Note Classic Vinyl BST 84194**](https://www.discogs.com/release/16941774) — Kevin Gray mastering, 180g, Optimal pressing. The recommended starting point for any listener.

**$150+:** The [**Music Matters 33rpm AAA mono**](https://www.discogs.com/release/7248785) — when available, this is the definitive listening copy. Seek the 2015 pressing specifically.

**$200–$500:** An original [**New York USA label stereo or mono first pressing**](https://www.discogs.com/release/4088476) with Van Gelder stamp and Plastylite ear. Understand that truly clean VG+ copies require careful inspection.
